George Brown College is a public college located in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. It was founded in 1967 and named after George Brown, a prominent 19th-century politician and newspaper publisher who played a significant role in the development of Canada.

The college offers over 160 programs in a wide range of fields, including business, community services, culinary arts, design, engineering technology, health sciences, hospitality, liberal arts, and technology. It has three main campuses: St. James Campus, Waterfront Campus, and Casa Loma Campus.


St. James Campus is located in the heart of downtown Toronto and is home to the School of Business, the School of Hospitality and Tourism Management, and the Centre for Health Sciences. Waterfront Campus, located on the shores of Lake Ontario, houses the Centre for Construction and Engineering Technologies, the School of Design, and the School of Media and Performing Arts. Casa Loma Campus is located in the historic Casa Loma neighbourhood and is home to the Centre for Preparatory and Liberal Studies, the Centre for Continuous Learning, and the School of Community Services.

George Brown College is known for its commitment to experiential learning and industry partnerships. The college has numerous partnerships with industry leaders in various fields, providing students with hands-on experience and opportunities to apply their knowledge and skills in real-world settings. Additionally, George Brown College has a strong focus on sustainability and social responsibility, and it has received numerous awards and accolades for its efforts in these areas.

Overall, George Brown College is an excellent institution for students seeking a high-quality education in a diverse and dynamic city.
